World History Guide
===================

World History explores the development of human societies across time and cultures.

Ancient Civilizations:
- Mesopotamia: "Cradle of Civilization," cuneiform writing
- Ancient Egypt: Pyramids, pharaohs, Nile River
- Indus Valley: Planned cities, advanced plumbing
- Ancient China: Dynasties, Great Wall, Silk Road
- Mesoamerica: Maya, Aztec, Olmec cultures

Classical Period:
- Ancient Greece: Democracy, philosophy, Olympics
- Roman Empire: Law, engineering, Pax Romana
- Han Dynasty China: Confucianism, bureaucracy
- Mauryan/Gupta India: Buddhism, mathematics

Middle Ages:
- Feudalism: Lords, vassals, serfs
- Byzantine Empire: Preserved Greek knowledge
- Islamic Golden Age: Science, mathematics, medicine
- Crusades: Religious wars between Christians and Muslims
- Black Death: Pandemic that killed millions

Renaissance and Reformation:
- Renaissance: Art, science, humanism (Italy)
- Scientific Revolution: Copernicus, Galileo, Newton
- Protestant Reformation: Martin Luther, religious change
- Age of Exploration: Columbus, Magellan, new trade routes

Age of Revolutions:
- American Revolution: Independence from Britain
- French Revolution: Liberty, equality, fraternity
- Industrial Revolution: Machines, factories, urbanization
- Latin American independence movements

19th Century Developments:
- Nationalism: Unification of Germany and Italy
- Imperialism: European colonization of Africa and Asia
- World Wars: WWI (1914-1918), WWII (1939-1945)
- Russian Revolution: Communism under Lenin
- Women's suffrage: Right to vote

20th Century Events:
- Cold War: USA vs USSR ideological conflict
- Decolonization: Independence of Asian and African nations
- Civil Rights Movement: Equality for African Americans
- Space Race: Moon landing, technological competition
- Fall of Berlin Wall: End of Cold War

Modern Era:
- Globalization: Increased interconnectedness
- Technology Revolution: Internet, computers, smartphones
- Climate Change: Environmental challenges
- Terrorism: 9/11 attacks and global response
- Economic Shifts: Rise of China, European Union

Historical Thinking Skills:
- Chronological Reasoning: Timeline of events
- Causation: Cause and effect relationships
- Continuity and Change: What stayed the same vs changed
- Contextualization: Understanding events in their time
- Argumentation: Making historical claims with evidence

Primary vs Secondary Sources:
- Primary: Original documents, artifacts, eyewitness accounts
- Secondary: Books, articles analyzing primary sources
- Bias: All sources have perspective
- Corroboration: Cross-checking multiple sources

Practice creating timelines and analyzing cause-and-effect relationships!
