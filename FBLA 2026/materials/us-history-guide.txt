US History Guide
===============

United States History covers the development of America from colonial times to the present.

Colonial Period (1607-1776):
- Jamestown (1607): First permanent English settlement
- Plymouth Colony (1620): Pilgrims and Mayflower Compact
- Thirteen Colonies: Massachusetts, New York, Virginia, etc.
- French and Indian War (1754-1763): Prelude to Revolution
- Declaration of Independence (1776): "Life, liberty, and pursuit of happiness"

American Revolution (1775-1783):
- Boston Tea Party (1773): Protest against British taxes
- Battles of Lexington and Concord (1775): "Shot heard 'round the world"
- George Washington: Commander of Continental Army
- Treaty of Paris (1783): Recognition of American independence
- Articles of Confederation: First US government

Constitutional Era (1787-1789):
- Constitutional Convention: Philadelphia, 1787
- Federalism: Division of power between states and federal government
- Bill of Rights: First 10 amendments protecting individual rights
- George Washington: First President of the United States

Early Republic (1789-1860):
- Louisiana Purchase (1803): Doubled size of United States
- War of 1812: "Second War of Independence"
- Monroe Doctrine (1823): Opposition to European colonization in Americas
- Industrial Revolution: Factory system and urbanization
- Abolition Movement: Fight against slavery

Civil War Era (1861-1865):
- Secession: Southern states leave Union
- Abraham Lincoln: President during Civil War
- Emancipation Proclamation (1863): Freed slaves in Confederate states
- Gettysburg Address: Lincoln's famous speech
- Reconstruction: Rebuilding the South after the war

Industrial Age (1865-1917):
- Westward Expansion: Manifest Destiny and frontier settlement
- Immigration: Millions arrive from Europe and Asia
- Progressive Era: Reforms for workers, women, and consumers
- Spanish-American War (1898): US becomes world power
- World War I (1917-1918): US entry and Treaty of Versailles

Great Depression and WWII (1929-1945):
- Stock Market Crash (1929): Economic collapse
- New Deal: FDR's programs to fight depression
- Pearl Harbor (1941): Japanese attack brings US into WWII
- D-Day (1944): Allied invasion of Normandy
- Atomic Bomb: End of WWII, beginning of Cold War

Post-War Era (1945-1991):
- Cold War: Ideological conflict with Soviet Union
- Civil Rights Movement: MLK, Rosa Parks, March on Washington
- Vietnam War (1965-1973): Controversial conflict
- Watergate Scandal (1972-1974): Nixon's resignation
- Space Race: Apollo 11 moon landing (1969)

Modern Era (1991-Present):
- End of Cold War: Fall of Soviet Union
- 9/11 Attacks (2001): War on Terror begins
- Great Recession (2008): Economic crisis and recovery
- Social Media Revolution: Digital age and connectivity
- Climate Change: Environmental challenges and solutions

Key Figures:
- George Washington: Revolutionary leader, first president
- Thomas Jefferson: Declaration author, third president
- Abraham Lincoln: Preserved Union, ended slavery
- Martin Luther King Jr.: Civil rights leader
- Franklin D. Roosevelt: New Deal, WWII leadership
- John F. Kennedy: Cuban Missile Crisis, space race
- Ronald Reagan: End of Cold War
- Barack Obama: First African American president

Major Documents:
- Declaration of Independence (1776)
- US Constitution (1787)
- Bill of Rights (1791)
- Gettysburg Address (1863)
- Civil Rights Act (1964)
- Voting Rights Act (1965)
